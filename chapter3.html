This chapter translates the clinical requirements identified in Chapter 2 into engineering design requirements for the Kinetix rehabilitation device. User needs were first identified through literature review and consultations with rehabilitation specialists.

3.1 Target Users & Demographics

For this project:

  • Client — stroke patients typically aged 40–80 undergoing lower-limb rehabilitation.
  • Physiotherapist — used broadly to include all rehabilitation specialists, such as occupational therapists and physiotherapists.

3.2 Anthropometric Data

To ensure the device accommodates the physical characteristics of the target population, anthropometric and biomechanical data were analysed to determine key design parameters.

Key anthropometric parameters of Singaporean adult males and females were referenced from Lee et al. (2019). Lower-limb length was estimated using segments 18 and 22, while foot dimensions were derived from segments 35 and 36.

3.2.3 Weight

Using data from Roystonn (2021), the mean weight of adult male Singaporeans is 72.7 ± 14.4 kg. In consideration of safety, the device is designed to withstand up to 150 kg, or roughly twice the mean weight.
